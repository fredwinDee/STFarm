Stone Terrace Farm

Forest Projects

The approximately 16 acres of wooded hillside on both sides of a valley are a designated Family Forest, previously known as a Tree Farm.

A small stream runs through the forest at about 80' in elevation below the farmhouse. This is a tributary to the West Branch of the Patapsco River so our small forest provides a much needed riparian buffer to this important feeder stream.

Project details:

  • Checkdams
  • Planting trees

Check dams

One of the significant current projects is an effort to construct check dams along the old logging roads created when the area was cut over about 60 years ago.

Planting trees

We have planted Sycamore trees in the small floodplain along the stream.

This is the view after clearing out various invasives such as Multiflora Rose , Japanese Barberry, and Oriental Bittersweet.

The Sycamores are planted in 5' tree shelters. These provide an advantageous greenhouse atmosphere for growth in the first 3-5 years and protects them from browsing by White-tailed deer.
